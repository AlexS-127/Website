Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovlace's father Lord Byron that was one of Britain's greatest poets but her mother wanted to stear her away so she would follow maths. And this plan worked, today Ada Lovelace is regarded to be the first author of a computer programme way before the modern computer even was a thing.

Ada invisioned machines that could manipulate symbols instead of just numbers and she is thought to be the first programmer and that really shows something when you consider she was taught by Charles Babbage.

Although Charles Babbage was 42 and Ada 17 they were both very smart. Babbages most notable invention was the analytical engine, it included a CPU (central processing unit) which he called the "Mill" and expandable memory which he called the "Store".

This machine was meant to carry out different mathematical equations depending on the input given. Ada imagined this machine to carry out more than just mathematical calculations ao she wrote out a programme for it, the first ever computer programme. It was made to calculate the sequence of numbers known as "Bernoulli Numbers", she made this programme on a diagram for the inputs.

Ada Lovlace theorised that this computer will evolve into something that is much grater, something that is capable of creating music and many more things. And as you know now this is now reality.
