Alan Turing

Alan Turing is a computer scientist who is famous for many things. For example he made the enigma decoder.

The enigma machine is an encryption device developed and used in the early- to mid-20th century to protect commercial, diplomatic and military communication. It was employed extensively by Nazi Germany during World War II.

This complex cypher machine worked by pressing a key on the keyboard in the front which encrypted this letter as another letter at the light board. An electrical signal was sent from the key through the plugboard at the front of the machine around through the 3 - 4 routers on the back and lit up a letter on the light board.

This meant the at the letter had a number of different paths thorugh the machine that would light up a number of different letters exept itself.

The enigma machine was first introduced to the public as a commercial sypher machine in the early 1920's but then later adopted by the German military system as their key encryption system.

The German military used dail sheets with the enigma machine key that would say which letters were to be connected where so when the message was to be delivered the reciever with the key could easily decypher it.

For you to configure the enigma machine to the correct state you would need to turn the routers at the back ot the correct positiona and also change the wiring on the plug board in the front.
